# Meta Ads Creative Fundamentals

Timeless principles for Meta advertising creative strategy. These apply regardless of trends or algorithm changes.

---

## Direct Response Principles

### AIDA — Attention, Interest, Desire, Action
- **Attention:** Hook that stops the scroll
- **Interest:** Relevant problem or insight that earns the next line
- **Desire:** Paint the outcome, build emotional investment
- **Action:** Clear, specific CTA

### PAS — Problem, Agitation, Solution
- **Problem:** Name the pain the audience feels
- **Agitation:** Twist the knife — make the cost of inaction visceral
- **Solution:** Present your product as the path out
- Best for: TOFU cold traffic, awareness campaigns

### BAB — Before, After, Bridge
- **Before:** Current painful state
- **After:** Desired transformed state
- **Bridge:** Your product is how they get there
- Best for: Transformation-focused offers, services, coaching

### Story-Based
- Open with a relatable moment or personal experience
- Build tension through a recognizable struggle
- Resolve with the product/service as the turning point
- Best for: UGC, testimonial-style ads, MOFU retargeting

### Listicle
- "3 reasons why..." or "5 mistakes that..."
- Each point builds the case
- Final point pivots to the product
- Best for: Carousel format, educational content, TOFU

## Hook Taxonomy

The hook is the single most important element. It determines whether the ad lives or dies.

### Pattern Interrupt
- Breaks expected scroll behavior
- Examples: "STOP. Don't buy [product] until you read this.", unexpected visual, jarring statement
- Risk: Can feel clickbaity if the body doesn't deliver
- Best for: TOFU, cold traffic

### Curiosity Gap
- Opens a knowledge gap the viewer needs to close
- Examples: "The #1 mistake people make with...", "What nobody tells you about..."
- Must be resolved in the ad — don't bait and switch
- Best for: TOFU/MOFU, educational angles

### Bold Claim
- Leads with a specific, impressive result
- Examples: "We 3x'd our client's ROAS in 14 days", "$47 → $4,700 in the first month"
- Must be substantiated (Meta policy + credibility)
- Best for: BOFU, social proof heavy campaigns

### Social Proof
- Leads with numbers, testimonials, or crowd validation
- Examples: "Join 100,000+ customers", "Rated 4.9 stars from 2,847 reviews"
- Specificity builds trust — exact numbers beat round ones
- Best for: MOFU/BOFU, e-commerce

### Us vs Them
- Positions your product against the old way, competitors, or conventional wisdom
- Examples: "Other brands use [cheap method]. We use [premium method]."
- Creates a clear differentiator in the viewer's mind
- Best for: Competitive markets, premium positioning

### Story Opening
- Starts mid-story to create immediate engagement
- Examples: "6 months ago I was $40k in debt...", "My doctor said I had 3 months..."
- Parasocial connection — viewer sees themselves in the story
- Best for: UGC, testimonial, transformation offers

### Question
- Asks something the target audience would say "yes" to
- Examples: "Tired of [pain point]?", "What if you could [desired outcome]?"
- The "yes" creates psychological commitment to keep reading
- Best for: TOFU, broad audiences

### Demonstration
- Shows the product in action immediately
- Examples: "Watch what happens when...", product unboxing, before/after
- Visual-first — works best in video and carousel
- Best for: E-commerce, physical products, Reels

### Controversy / Hot Take
- Opens with an opinion that challenges norms
- Examples: "Unpopular opinion: [industry thing] is a scam", "I'll say what no one will..."
- Drives engagement (comments, shares) which boosts reach
- Best for: TOFU, brand awareness, thought leadership

### Before / After
- Visual or narrative contrast between two states
- Must comply with Meta policies (no misleading transformations)
- Side-by-side or sequential format
- Best for: Services, coaching, beauty, fitness, home improvement

## Funnel Stage Creative Strategy

### TOFU — Top of Funnel (Cold Traffic)
- **Objective:** Awareness, reach, video views
- **Audience:** Never heard of you. Skeptical. Scrolling fast.
- **Hook approach:** Pattern interrupt, curiosity gap, question, controversy
- **Copy length:** Short to medium. Earn trust fast or lose them.
- **Visual:** Eye-catching, native-feeling, UGC-style often outperforms polished
- **CTA:** Soft — "Learn more", "See how it works", "Watch this"
- **Key metric:** Hook rate, CPM, ThruPlay rate

### MOFU — Middle of Funnel (Warm Traffic)
- **Objective:** Consideration, engagement, traffic
- **Audience:** Knows you exist. Interested but not convinced. Comparing options.
- **Hook approach:** Social proof, story, us vs them, demonstration
- **Copy length:** Medium to long. They'll read more because they're already interested.
- **Visual:** Product in context, testimonials, comparison content
- **CTA:** Medium — "Shop now", "Get the details", "See pricing"
- **Key metric:** CTR, CPC, engagement rate

### BOFU — Bottom of Funnel (Hot Traffic)
- **Objective:** Conversion, sales, leads
- **Audience:** Ready to buy. Needs a final push. Overcoming last objections.
- **Hook approach:** Bold claim, social proof, urgency, before/after
- **Copy length:** Can be short — they already know you. Focus on the offer.
- **Visual:** Product close-ups, testimonials, offer-focused, countdown
- **CTA:** Hard — "Buy now", "Claim your discount", "Book your call"
- **Key metric:** CPA, ROAS, cost per lead, conversion rate

## Video Ad Structure

### Short-Form (Reels / Stories — 15-30s)
```
0-3s    HOOK — Pattern interrupt, text overlay, movement
3-8s    PROBLEM — Name the pain, show the struggle
8-18s   SOLUTION — Show the product, demonstrate the result
18-25s  PROOF — Testimonial, results, social proof
25-30s  CTA — Clear ask, urgency if appropriate
```

### Medium-Form (Feed Video — 30-60s)
```
0-3s    HOOK — Strongest visual + text hook
3-10s   PROBLEM — Agitate the pain point with specifics
10-25s  SOLUTION — Product demo, feature walkthrough, transformation
25-40s  PROOF — Multiple proof points (reviews, numbers, before/after)
40-55s  OBJECTION HANDLE — Address the #1 hesitation
55-60s  CTA — Tell them exactly what to do next
```

### Key Video Rules
- Sound-off first design — use captions and text overlays always
- First frame must be compelling (thumbnail in feed)
- Vertical (9:16) for Stories/Reels, square (1:1) or 4:5 for Feed
- Movement in first 1 second — static opens get scrolled past
- Cut rhythm: new visual every 2-3 seconds to maintain attention

## Meta Platform Specs

### Character Limits
| Element | Visible | Max |
|---------|---------|-----|
| Primary text | 125 chars (before "See More") | 2,200 chars |
| Headline | 40 chars | 255 chars |
| Description | 30 chars | 255 chars |

### Image Specs
| Placement | Ratio | Min Resolution |
|-----------|-------|---------------|
| Feed | 1:1 or 4:5 | 1080x1080 or 1080x1350 |
| Stories / Reels | 9:16 | 1080x1920 |
| Right column | 1.91:1 | 1200x628 |
| Carousel (each card) | 1:1 | 1080x1080 |

### Video Specs
| Placement | Ratio | Length |
|-----------|-------|--------|
| Feed | 1:1 or 4:5 | 15-60s (sweet spot: 15-30s) |
| Stories | 9:16 | 5-15s per card |
| Reels | 9:16 | 15-90s (sweet spot: 15-30s) |
| In-stream | 16:9 or 1:1 | 5-15s |

### Format Use Cases
| Format | Best For |
|--------|---------|
| Single image | Simple message, clear product, fast production |
| Carousel | Multi-product, storytelling, listicle, step-by-step |
| Video / Reels | Demonstration, UGC, testimonials, complex products |
| Stories | Urgency, flash sales, behind-the-scenes, polls |
| Collection | E-commerce catalog, product discovery |

## Meta Ad Policies — Key Rules

### Prohibited Content
- Misleading or false claims
- Before/after images implying unrealistic results
- Personal attributes: Don't assert or imply characteristics ("Are you overweight?" is rejected)
- Fake UI elements (fake play buttons, fake notifications, fake close buttons)
- Sensationalist or shocking content
- Surveillance or spyware-related products

### Special Ad Categories
These have restricted targeting (no age, gender, zip code targeting) AND stricter creative rules:
- **Housing** — Real estate, mortgages, home insurance
- **Credit** — Credit cards, loans, financial services
- **Employment** — Job listings, recruitment
- **Social/Political** — Campaigns, advocacy, ballot measures

### Claims That Need Substantiation
- Income or financial results
- Health or weight loss outcomes
- Performance statistics
- "Guaranteed" anything
- Comparative claims ("better than X")

### Safe Practices
- Use "may help" instead of "will cure"
- Show realistic outcomes with disclaimers
- Avoid second-person personal attributes — use third-person ("People who..." not "You are...")
- Keep text on images reasonable (Meta penalizes heavy text)
- Ensure landing page matches ad claims

## Offer Framing

### E-Commerce Offers
- **Percentage off:** "Save 30%" — works best for higher-priced items
- **Dollar off:** "Save $50" — works best when the number feels significant
- **BOGO:** "Buy 1, Get 1 Free" — increases AOV
- **Free shipping:** Often more motivating than a small discount
- **Bundle:** "Get the complete set for $X" — increases perceived value
- **Limited time:** Creates urgency — must be truthful
- **Limited stock:** Creates scarcity — must be truthful

### Service-Based Offers
- **Free consultation / audit:** Low commitment entry point
- **Free trial / first session free:** Reduces risk
- **Money-back guarantee:** Removes purchase anxiety
- **Payment plan:** Reduces perceived cost barrier
- **Bonus stacking:** "Sign up and also get X, Y, Z"
- **Results-based framing:** "Get [outcome] or your money back"

### Offer Presentation Rules
- Lead with the value, not the price
- Anchor high, then reveal the deal
- Stack the value: "You get X ($200 value), Y ($150 value), and Z ($100 value) — all for $97"
- Always have a reason for the offer (launch, anniversary, seasonal) — "because" increases compliance
- Urgency must be real. Fake countdown timers destroy trust.

## Ad Fatigue — Signals and Remedies

### Fatigue Signals
| Signal | Threshold | Meaning |
|--------|-----------|---------|
| Frequency > 2.5 | Warning | Same people seeing it too often |
| CTR declining 20%+ | Action needed | Audience is ignoring the ad |
| CPA rising 30%+ | Action needed | Efficiency degrading |
| Hook rate declining | Early warning | Creative is losing stopping power |
| Comment sentiment shifting negative | Action needed | Audience is actively annoyed |

### Refresh Strategies (Least to Most Effort)
1. **New headline/primary text** — Same visual, new copy angle (1 hour)
2. **New hook** — Same body and CTA, new opening line (1 hour)
3. **New thumbnail / first frame** — Same video, different opening visual (2 hours)
4. **New format** — Same message, different format (image → carousel, video → static) (half day)
5. **New angle** — Same product, completely different creative approach (1 day)
6. **New concept** — Start fresh with new creative direction (2-3 days)

### Refresh Rules
- Don't kill a fatigued ad that was a winner — pause it, refresh, relaunch later
- Keep a "creative bench" — have 2-3 tested concepts ready to rotate in
- Scale horizontally (more creatives) not just vertically (more budget to one creative)
- Creative shelf life on Meta: typically 2-6 weeks before fatigue hits (varies by audience size)

## Creative Testing Methodology

### Testing Hierarchy
1. **Concept / Angle** — Test first. Biggest impact on performance.
2. **Hook** — Test second. Determines if the ad gets seen.
3. **Format** — Test third. Determines engagement pattern.
4. **Body copy** — Test fourth. Refines persuasion.
5. **CTA** — Test last. Smallest incremental impact.

### Testing Rules
- One variable per test
- Minimum 3 days before making decisions
- Minimum spend threshold before judging ($50-100 per variant minimum)
- Use a control (current best performer) in every test
- Kill losers when confidence is clear, don't wait for the test end date
- Document every test result — wins AND losses

### Statistical Confidence
- **Quick gut check:** If one variant has 2x the results of another after meaningful spend, you can probably call it
- **Proper confidence:** 95% confidence needs ~100+ conversions per variant for conversion-based tests
- **Small budget reality:** You won't always get statistical significance. Use directional data + pattern matching from swipe file
